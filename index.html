J.R.R. Tolkein

One of the most influential authors of all time

  • 1892 - John Ronald Reuel Tolkien is born in Bloemfontein, South Africa.
  • 1896 - His father dies of rheumatic fever.
  • 1911 - Travels to the Switzerland Alps, where he draws inspiration for Bilbo's journey in The Lord Of The Rings. He formed a secret club with three of his friends called "Tea Club and Barrovian Society". This club played a great role in his future passion for literature. He started studying at Oxford's University.
  • 1915 - He enlisted in the British Army.
  • 1916 - Marries wife Edith Mary Bratt.
  • 1920 - Leaves the Army. Starts teaching at the Leeds University.
  • 1925 - Starts teaching at Oxford University.
  • 1926 - Finishes translation of Beowulf, which he had been working on for at least three years.
  • 1937 - Publishes "Beowulf: The Monsters and Critics" book. He also later publishes "The Hobbit", which becomes an immediate success. He proceeds to start working in the sequel, "The Fellowship of the Ring".
  • 1947 - He publishes "On Fairy Stories", which is an adaptation of a collection of known fantasy stories.
  • 1954 - He publishes "Fellowship of the Ring", the first book in the "Lord of the Rings" trilogy. He later publishes "The Two Towers", the second installment in the "Lord of the Rings" trilogy.
  • 1955 - He publishes "Return of the King", the third and last book on the "Lord of the Rings" trilogy.
  • 1972 - Is appointed a Commander of the Order of the British Empire by Queen Elizabeth.
  • 1973 - He dies at the age of 81, shortly after the death of his wife Edith.
